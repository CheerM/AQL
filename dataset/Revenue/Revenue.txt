IBM Reports 2006 Fourth-Quarter Results

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARMONK, NY - 18 Jan 2007: 

Total revenues of $26.3 billion, up 7 percent as reported; 
Diluted earnings of $2.26 per share from continuing operations, up 12 percent as reported, or 7 percent compared with the fourth-quarter 2005, excluding pension curtailment charge; 
Services signings of $17.8 billion, up 55 percent. 
IBM today announced fourth-quarter 2006 diluted earnings of $2.26 per share from continuing operations, an increase of 12 percent as reported, compared with diluted earnings of $2.01 per share in the fourth quarter of 2005; the fourth-quarter 2005 diluted earnings include $0.10 per share for a one-time pretax curtailment charge of $267 million related to pension changes.

From a geographic perspective, the Americas fourth-quarter revenues were $11.1 billion, an increase of 6 percent as reported (5 percent, adjusting for currency) from the 2005 period. Revenues from Europe/Middle East/Africa were $9.3 billion, up 11 percent (3 percent, adjusting for currency). Asia-Pacific revenues increased 7 percent (5 percent, adjusting for currency) to $4.8 billion. OEM revenues were $1.0 billion, down 3 percent compared with the 2005 fourth quarter. Revenues from the Software segment were $5.6 billion.
